## Justification for non-coverage

Operationalizes MITRE criterion RR-4 (Coverage Justification):

- **C/C++ memory-safety CWEs** (CWE-787, -125, -416, -119, -476, -190) are out of scope — they require runtime / pointer-arithmetic analysis that JS/TS source doesn't expose. Belongs in CodeQL / Coverity.
- **Information-disclosure CWEs** (CWE-200) typically need data-flow / taint tracking which Interlace doesn't do natively — partial coverage exists in `secure-coding/no-leaked-error-message` etc.
- **Authorization-logic CWEs** (CWE-862, -863, -269) are mostly *application semantics* not detectable from generic AST patterns — covered in `nestjs-security/*` and `express-security/*` for framework-specific patterns only.
- **Resource-exhaustion CWEs** (CWE-400) overlap with `eslint-plugin-regexp` (ReDoS) — partial coverage; full coverage requires pattern complexity analysis we plan to add.
